# The Bounded Agent — Manifesto

> An agent is not limited by its reasoning power. It is limited by what enters
> its attention budget at each step. Everything below follows from that.

When you configure an agent, you make the same decision over and over: what goes
in, what stays out, what loads when. These are the trade-offs we favor.

We have come to value:

- **Curated context** over **maximal context**
- **External structure** over **in-context memory**
- **Just-in-time loading** over **just-in-case loading**
- **A compact, stable core** over **a complete one**
- **Principles** over **rules**
- **Routing to the task** over **roles bound to the agent**
- **Traceable decisions** over **exhaustive documentation**
- **Recycled effort** over **repeated effort**

That is, while there is value in the items on the right, we weight the items
on the left more.

These are preferences, not arguments. The reasoning, the evidence, and the
honest labels on that evidence live in [`principles/`](principles/) and
[`concepts/`](concepts/). The manifesto only names the choice.
